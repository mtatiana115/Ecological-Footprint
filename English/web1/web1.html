Carbon Footprint

The carbon footprint is a way to measure the impact we have on the planet. Every time we do something that uses energy, such as traveling, eating, or using our phone, we are emitting gases that contribute to global warming.

These gases are called greenhouse gases, and they accumulate in the atmosphere, causing the Earth's temperature to rise. The carbon footprint is expressed in tons or kilos of carbon dioxide, which is the most abundant and harmful gas.

How do we know if we are emitting more carbon dioxide than the planet can absorb?

A creative and simple way to explain why the carbon footprint is expressed in terms of the number of planets is to imagine that each person has a backpack where they store all the carbon dioxide they emit by doing their activities. Carbon dioxide is a gas that contributes to global warming and is produced when using energy, such as electricity, transportation, or food. Every time we use energy, we fill our backpack with more carbon dioxide.

Some people have bigger backpacks than others, depending on their lifestyle and their country. For example, the environmental NGO The Nature Conservancy estimates that each inhabitant of the planet generates an average of almost four tons of CO2 annually, while in countries like the United States, this amount quadruples per person per year.

Now, the planet has a limited capacity to absorb the carbon dioxide we emit. Trees, plants, and oceans act as sponges that capture some of this gas and transform it into oxygen. But if we emit more carbon dioxide than the planet can absorb, we are saturating the atmosphere and causing the Earth's temperature to rise. This has serious consequences for the climate, biodiversity, and people's health.

This is where the concept of the carbon footprint in planets comes in. The carbon footprint in planets is a way to compare our carbon dioxide backpack with the planet's capacity to absorb it. It is calculated by dividing the amount of carbon dioxide we emit by the amount of carbon dioxide the planet can absorb. The result tells us how many Earths we would need if everyone had our same carbon dioxide backpack. For example, if our carbon footprint in planets is 1, it means that we are emitting the same amount of carbon dioxide that the planet can absorb. But if our carbon footprint in planets is 2, it means that we are emitting twice as much carbon dioxide as the planet can absorb, and we would need two Earths to offset our emissions. And so on.

The carbon footprint in planets helps us become aware of our impact on the environment and look for ways to reduce it. Some actions we can take to reduce our carbon footprint include using less energy, recycling, consuming local and eco-friendly products, and using environmentally friendly means of transportation. This way, we can take care of the planet and prevent climate change from becoming irreversible.

How is it calculated?

To calculate the carbon footprint using utility bill information, you can follow a simple formula that is based on multiplying the consumption of each service by a corresponding emission factor. The emission factor is the amount of carbon dioxide generated for each unit of consumption.

In this way, the results are added to the household's carbon footprint. This will provide a more comprehensive estimate of the amount of greenhouse gases emitted from the use of public services.
